# EJNMMI main submission checklist

Target journal: European Journal of Nuclear Medicine and Molecular Imaging.

Article type: Original Article.

Positioning: field-level identifiability and feasibility limit for positronium-lifetime hypoxia imaging in nuclear medicine.

Submission package generated by `./run_all.sh`:

- `manuscript_ejnmmi.docx` — main editable manuscript.
- `manuscript_ejnmmi.pdf` — reviewer PDF.
- `cover_letter_ejnmmi.docx` / `.pdf`.
- `ESM_1_monte_carlo_validation.pdf` — supplementary online resource.
- `figures/Fig1.png` to `figures/Fig6.png` — high-resolution figure files.
- `ESM_1_monte_carlo_validation.png` — supplementary figure source.

Format checks:

- Structured abstract uses Purpose, Methods, Results, Conclusion.
- Keywords: 6.
- Main text uses Introduction, Materials and methods, Results, Discussion, References.
- Declarations are placed after References.
- Figures are cited in consecutive order.
- Supplement is cited as Online Resource 1.
- Main figure files are named using the Fig-number convention.
- Figures are generated at 600 dpi and have no embedded figure titles.
- Reference citations use numbered square brackets and now follow first-appearance order.
- DOI links are included where available; remaining preprints are labelled explicitly as arXiv/medRxiv records.
- Count terminology distinguishes total in-window events (`N_total`) from o-Ps counts (`N3`).
- The Mercolli 44Sc yield is explicitly framed as a public-preprint scenario anchor, not as a published scanner constant.

Scientific caveat to keep explicit:

- The Monte Carlo validates estimator attainability under the assumed fixed-nuisance spectral model.
- It does not validate the biological constants: `kappa_lipid`, `dI3/df`, or `dI3/dpO2`.
- The decisive empirical anchor remains PALS O2 x water/lipid/protein or real-data I3 re-analysis.

Editorial risk:

- EJNMMI main is a stretch target because this is a modelling/methods study without new clinical data.
- The likely transfer destination, if not accepted at main EJNMMI, is EJNMMI Physics.
